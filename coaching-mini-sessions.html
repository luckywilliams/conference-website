Coaching Mini Sessions

Saturday, March 24

Use this opportunity to get a 25 minute one-on-one session with a leadership coach - get practical tips that will help you grow and tackle your challenges. Sign ups for spots will happen on-site.

Sign up for a session with:

Laura Close

Executive leadership coach and career consultant Laura Close sees a select portfolio of professionals and entrepreneurs open to the transformative work of leadership development. Seattle clients book private sessions in her Pioneer Square office and remote clients see her live on Skype. Laura’s first book is due out in 2019. Learn more at LauraClose.com.

You'll have the chance to book your free 25 minute consultation with Laura on-site at Tech Women Rising. During your private mini Laura will listen to your topic area and offer frameworks and tips from her private practice that you can apply to growing your influence, leadership, career or business ownership trajectory. From management to negotiations to client acquisition she is ready to listen.

Janet Andrews

Janet has over 20 years of experience as a coach, consultant and business leader with emphasis on organizational strategy and change, and leadership development. She believes that high performing organizations and impacts start with individual leadership, no matter what role/level a person is within an organization. Janet helps leaders transition into new roles, maximize their opportunity, and build more resilient teams and programs. Learn more at Consociata.

You’ll have the chance to book a free first-come, first-serve 25 minute coaching session with Janet. If you are preparing for a crucial conversation, learning how to be a more effective team leader, or figuring out your team dynamics and how to be more influential as a team player, Janet will be a sounding board around your challenge and objective. She’ll discuss with you ideas and possible strategies based on her work consulting to, being on, and leading teams.

Janice Lichtenwaldt

Janice spent 25 years fighting the good fight to keep sanity, creativity, and productivity in the emerging technology space (marketing, communications, product development, business development) before founding her leadership and career coaching practice. She is a passionate advocate for women leaders and helped found women’s leadership programs at both Expedia and T-Mobile. Janice’s mission is to help others, especially women, recognize their potential and achieve their definition of success. Learn more at ActuallyICan.coach.

Tech Women Rising is making Janice’s services available all day Saturday for participants. Sign up for free, 25-minute coaching sessions on a first come, first serve basis. Bring a topic or issue you’ve been wrestling with, a goal you’re struggling to achieve, or an idea you’ve been wanting to pursue and leave the mini session with more clarity and at least one next step toward your definition of success.
